Your Memories

What are memories?

As you work with Thais, she quietly learns about you — your goals, projects, preferences, and working style. These become memories that carry forward into every future session, so you never have to re-explain yourself.

How are they created?

Thais extracts memories automatically after each conversation. You never need to say "remember this." She identifies what's worth keeping and what isn't. If something seems ambiguous, she may ask a clarifying question.

Confidence levels

Each memory has a confidence level that reflects how certain Thais is about it.


high Confirmed through multiple mentions or direct statements.

medium Observed once with reasonable certainty.

low Inferred or ambiguous — held lightly.

Memory types

Memories are classified by what they represent:


goal project preference skill decision observation emotional_pattern

What's coming

In the future, memories will be compressed and archived over time — keeping only what remains useful. Low-value memories will fade. Patterns that repeat will strengthen. The goal is a system that feels helpful, not one that just accumulates.

Your control

You can delete any memory permanently at any time. Thais will never reference something you've removed. This is your workspace — the memory belongs to you.
